# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary user: a person with diabetes who wears an Abbott FreeStyle Libre CGM and sleeps with their phone nearby. They are often woken at night by low- or high-glucose events, may be groggy, stressed, or operating on poor sleep. Secondary users include family members or caregivers who might glance at the device. The app is used in high-stakes, low-bandwidth moments, so every screen must be readable at a glance.

## Product Purpose

FreeStyle Libre Alarm turns the official FreeStyle Libre app's quiet notifications into a loud, full-screen, impossible-to-miss alarm. It exists to prevent missed glucose events during sleep — when a silent notification can become dangerous. Success means the user never misses a critical glucose alert because the notification was too quiet.

## Brand Personality

Loved-one companion: personal, caring, calm, and clear. The app was built for a friend, and it should feel like it — warm enough to be human, restrained enough to never add anxiety. No clinical coldness, no consumer-fitness playfulness, no dark-mode gaming drama. The voice is quietly confident: "I'm watching, and I'll wake you up if you need me."

## Anti-references

- Hospital-clinical coldness: sterile blues, dense data tables, intimidating medical language.
- Consumer-fitness playfulness: neon gradients, gamified streaks, trophy language.
- Dark-mode gaming aesthetics: high-contrast neon, aggressive reds, sci-fi chrome.
- AI/SaaS marketing tropes: cream-and-coral editorial palettes, slab-serif display type, abstract product mockups (the existing DESIGN.md describes Claude.com, not this app).

## Design Principles

1. **Safety-first legibility.** The most important information must be readable at 3 AM, at arm's length, under stress. Contrast, type size, and spacing are safety features.
2. **Calm urgency.** Alarm surfaces are unmissable without inducing panic. Red is reserved for real danger; the rest of the app stays composed.
3. **Loved-one clarity.** Every label and action explains itself. No medical jargon, no hidden states, no "figure it out" settings.
4. **One clear action per screen.** Monitoring is about one thing: is the alarm on? History is about one thing: what happened and when? Settings group related choices, but each card has a single job.
5. **Restraint over novelty.** No decorative motion, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no AI slop scaffolding. The interface gets out of the way until it is needed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline platform accessibility: supports system text sizing, respects dark mode, uses color-independent indicators for status, and keeps touch targets at or above platform minimums. The app runs in nighttime, high-stress contexts, so legibility and predictability are treated as core product requirements even when no formal certification is targeted.
